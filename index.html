Publications

To Think Like a Vertex (or Not) for Distributed Training of Graph Neural Networks

CCGRID '23

Graph Neural Networks (GNNs) train neural networks that combine the topological properties of a graph with the vertex and edge features to perform tasks such as node classification and link prediction. We propose a novel middleware that approaches GNN training from the perspective of a vertex-centric model (VCM) of distributed graph processing and overlays neural network training over it. Giraph Graph Neural Network (G2N2) uses a three-phase execution pattern by construction a distributed computation graph per mini-batch, and maps the forward and backward passes of the GNN training to VCM. We implement a prototype of G2N2 in Apache Giraph and report results from a preliminary evaluation using two real-world graphs on a commodity cluster. Paper

Performance Modelling of Graph Neural Networks

CCGRID '23

We formulate the calculation of the Floating Point Operations (FLOPs) required for a single forward pass through layers of a GNN. We report the analytical calculations for GraphConv and GraphSAGE models and compare against their profiling results for 10 graphs with varying properties. We observe that there is a strong correlation between our theoretical expectation of the number of FLOPs and the experimental execution time for a forward pass.

Seizure Detection from Intracranial Electroencephalography Recordings

AISC, Volume 1166, '20

In our research, we have tried to design an early warning system for such patients, which could inform them of an increased probability of a seizure attack in the near future, giving them an opportunity to move to an accommodating setup. This forecasting system required the correct classification of runtime Intracranial Electroencephalography (iEEG) signal segments into interictal and preictal segments. Detection of a preictal segment serving as the required warning.
